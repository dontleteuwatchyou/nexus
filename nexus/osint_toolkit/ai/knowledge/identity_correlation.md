# Identity correlation and attribution

Le même pseudo sur plusieurs sites ne prouve jamais la même identité. Une URL
de profil construite automatiquement ne confirme ni l'existence du compte, ni
son propriétaire. Un nom identique, un pseudo identique ou une URL probable
reste une piste tant que plusieurs attributs indépendants ne concordent pas.

An exact username match is not an identity match. Common handles such as
`yanis`, `alex`, `sam` or `admin` can belong to unrelated people on every
platform. A generated profile URL is only a candidate until the page is
retrieved and its public content is inspected.

Use an attribution matrix. Compare independent attributes such as a stable
profile identifier, linked domain, self-declared name, profile image, location,
biography, creation timeline and cross-links published by the accounts
themselves. Two weak attributes copied from one source do not become two
independent proofs.

Confidence vocabulary:

- `CONFIRMÉ`: direct, reproducible evidence explicitly links the asset and
  subject.
- `PROBABLE`: at least two independent, meaningful attributes agree and no
  material contradiction is present.
- `PISTE`: one match or automated detector; manual validation is required.
- `NON ATTRIBUÉ`: the identifier matches but ownership cannot be established.
- `RÉFUTÉ`: reliable evidence contradicts the proposed attribution.

Never merge profiles merely because the display name or username matches.
Record contradictions and alternative explanations. Ask for a more
discriminating pivot—an authorised email, known handle, public profile URL or
domain—when a name is too broad.

Report the queried identifier separately from the claimed real-world person.
The safe conclusion for a generic username is normally: “These are candidate
accounts using the identifier; attribution to the named person is impossible
with the current evidence.”
